Point de Charge

A proposal for a streetlight-mounted socket for charging electric vehicles for Lausanne, in anticipation of the 2030 ban on non-electric vehicles.


The design seeks to be compact with an appropriate level of visibility on the street, and references the familiarity and durability of the cast street signage found throughout the city.

Fold for Schätti

A light realised at ECAL in collaboration with Schätti Leuchten, a Swiss metal fabrication and lighting company. A central hinge – the first item ever produced by the company – allows the light to be folded and stored flat for convenient transport.


Fold was exhibited by Schätti and ECAL at the 2023 Salone di Mobile.

Cliffhanger

A proposal for a wind turbine situated on the island of Fogo, situated off the coast of Newfoundland, Canada. In place of a conventional underground concrete foundation, the structure would be tethered to the landscape with horizontal struts.


The removal of concrete is environmentally advantageous, with the form also referencing the traditional fishing huts on the island which were built on stilts.


Cliffhanger was exhibited by ECAL at the 2023 Salone di Mobile.

U.F.O.G.O

When visiting Fogo island we had a short workshop with the brief to design a flying object.


Juniper trees populate the windswept island and are frequently and sharply bent by the force of the wind. This curved section was traditionally used in the bows of fishing boats; we repurposed the material as a Frisbee.

Designed with compact living spaces in mind. Desk organiser with a clipping mechanism for attachment to the side of a flat surface; the caddy adds space instead of occupying it.

Heat Sink Cups

Two double walled slip-cast porcelain cups, designed to provide insulation to the holding hand.


The intent was to create a tension between the expectancy that a thin walled vessel will readily transmit heat and the insulating properties of the air gap.
